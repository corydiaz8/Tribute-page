A Timeline of John Lewis' life

  • 1940- Lewis was born near Troy, Alabama.
  • 1955- At the age of 15, he delivers his first public sermon.
  • 1957- John Lewis first meets Rosa Parks (notourious for her courageous Bus Boycott) and Dr. Martin Luther King a year later.
  • 1961- Along with six other black members and six white members, Lewis became one of the original freedom riders to push for desegregation on interstate buses in the south.
  • 1963- He is elected chairman of the Student Non-violent Coordinating Committee where he would peacefully but loudly fight for equality and speak with Dr. Martin Luther King in the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom. He would also head projects like the Freedom Summer Project in Mississippi, where he would aid in registering local minority communities to vote.
  • 1966- Lewis would move to New York to become associate director of the Field Foundation and move back to Atlanta to lead the Southern Regional Council's Community Organization Project.
  • 1970- For seven years, John Lewis would be the director of the Voter Education Project. Through this role, he would get almost a total of four million minority voters registered and give them acesss to voter mobilization tours.
  • 1977- John Lewis for the very first time decides to run for congresss. After being defeated in his first election, he takes upon another government role within the Carter administration.
  • 1986- After defeating Julian Bond in the democratic primary, Lewis then went on to win his second Georgia senate race with a huge 75%-25% win in the vote.
  • 1988-2018- Lewis was re-elected 16 times to the US senate. Staying above 70% throughout almost all of his elections, Lewis had even gone unopposed in some races.
  • 2013- Along with comic novel writer Andrew Aydin, Lewis creates a new comic book series called March. In the series, Lewis portrays the events of the Civil Rights Movement.
  • 2016- Since 1988, Lewis has pushed for a bill to create a National African American Museum in Washington D.C. The bill was finally passed in 2003 and then signed by president Bush the same year. The museum (neighboring the Washington Memorial) held its opening ceremony on September 25. 2016.
  • 2019- Lewis is diagnosed with pancreatic cancer. "I have never faced a fight quite like the one I have now," says Lewis as he battles with Cancer for 18 months.
  • 2020- On July 17, 2020, Lewis passes away. Praised by many people not just across the country , but across the world, many payed their respects to the people's championed Civil Rights Leader as he Lie in State in the Nation's Capitol Rotunda.
